I am an Affiliate at the University of Cambridge and a Fellow of the University of London. I am an alumnus of the prestigious Sir Peter Mansfield Imaging Centre in Nottingham, UK, which is the birthplace of MRI. I finished my Ph.D. in Computational Neuroscience from the Otto-von-Guericke University, Germany and M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Nebraska, USA. Previously I also served as a Technical Officer of the Indian Brain Grid Project at the National Neuroimaging Facility, India.

My research is centered on Neuroimaging, specifically Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI). I am particularly interested in Ultra-high field human fMRI and the application of machine learning and other computational modelling in understanding how the visual and tactile information are represented in different parts of the brain. For more details on my research, please have a look at my publications. I am always looking forward to collaborate on interesting ideas in MR Imaging, please drop me an email if you have one.

Apart from research, I love to play music, travel unplanned, eat hot and spicy Indian food and make new friends.
